BUSINESS SUBJECT DOSSIER
=======================

Subject Label: NovaFlow SaaS - B2B Workflow Automation - Q1 2026
System Type: Company (SaaS scale-up)
Evaluation Date: 2026-03-21
Data Source: Internal KPI snapshot + board summary

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Assess operational stability before expansion to 3 new EU markets.
- Decide whether to prioritize growth or system hardening over next 2 quarters.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. FORM EVIDENCE (Identity / Integrity over Time)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Mission clarity: Strong product thesis (automation for regulated SMB workflows).
- Product architecture: Monolith with growing service boundaries, technical debt rising.
- Team structure: 82 FTE, high dependency on 6 senior engineers.
- Governance: Quarterly planning exists, but incident postmortems are inconsistent.
- Security baseline: SOC2 in progress, 61% controls implemented.

Trend:
- Product-market fit improved over last 3 quarters, but internal complexity increased.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. POSITION EVIDENCE (Context / Fit / Placement)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Market segment: Mid-market SMBs in DACH + CEE, compliance-heavy verticals.
- Competitive pressure: 2 large incumbents lowered pricing by 12% in Q4 2025.
- Channel health: 38% of new deals via partner channel, partner churn increased.
- Regulatory context: New data residency demands in 2 target markets.
- Customer concentration: Top 10 customers = 41% of ARR (concentration risk).

Trend:
- Good category fit, but external pressure and concentration risk are rising.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. ACTION EVIDENCE (Execution / Output / Energy Cost)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- ARR: EUR 7.4M (+34% YoY).
- Net Revenue Retention: 108% (down from 116% last year).
- Gross margin: 72% (stable).
- Burn multiple: 1.9 (target <1.5).
- EBITDA margin: -12% (improved from -18%).
- Deployment frequency: 21 deploys/week (good), change failure rate 12% (high).
- MTTR: 4.8h average (target <2h).
- Support SLA breaches: 9.6% tickets in Q1 (target <3%).

Trend:
- Output growth remains strong, but execution energy cost is high and reliability drifts.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. RISKS / HEADWINDS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Key-person risk in core engineering and enterprise sales.
- Reliability debt may convert to churn if SLA misses persist.
- Expansion may amplify support and compliance load beyond current capacity.
- Customer concentration increases downside in one-contract-loss scenarios.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. INTERVENTIONS / SCENARIOS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Scenario A - Growth-first:
- Open 3 markets in parallel, maintain current engineering split.
- Expected effect: higher ARR growth, but likely reliability deterioration.

Scenario B - Stabilize-first:
- Pause one market launch, move 25% roadmap capacity to reliability/compliance.
- Expected effect: lower short-term growth, stronger Form/Action stability.

Scenario C - Hybrid:
- Launch 2 markets, enforce reliability gate (SLA+MTTR) before 3rd launch.
- Expected effect: balanced growth and controlled operational risk.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Missing: full cohort retention by segment and root-cause coding for incidents.
- Assumption: macro demand remains stable in core verticals through 2026.
