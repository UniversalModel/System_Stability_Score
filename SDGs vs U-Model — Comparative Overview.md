Introduction

This report compares two governance architectures with global ambitions: (A) the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)‚Äîa 2015 UN agreement of 17 goals and 169 targets guiding public and private action to 2030‚Äîand (B) the U-Model (Universal Model of Sustainable Governance)‚Äîa triadic performance system that evaluates entities using three groups of principles: Code (Refusal to Harm), Credo (Organizational Benefit), and Rights (Correctness of Expectations). Together, these three groups assess an entity‚Äôs contribution to five Main Goals: Minimizing Public Costs, Maximizing Productivity and Efficiency, Maximizing Service to Citizens, Minimizing Mortality, and Maximizing Happiness. Also see the U-Model core documents for Code , Credo , Rights , and the Five Main Goals .

What they are (at a glance)

SDGs: Outcome blueprint (ends). A UN-wide agenda (2015‚Äì2030) that defines what the world should achieve, monitored by globally aligned indicators (e.g., poverty, health, education, environment).

U-Model: Operating system (means + measurement). Scores how people/organizations govern and deliver value across Code‚ÄìCredo‚ÄìRights, aligned to the Five Main Goals.

Methodological differences (why they‚Äôre complementary)

Scope: SDGs are global targets (the destination). U-Model is a governance scorecard (how capable/ethical the driver is).

Granularity: SDGs track outcomes (e.g., poverty rate). U-Model evaluates behaviors/systems (e.g., anti-corruption, clarity of roles, employee rights).

Actionability: SDGs can say what to improve; U-Model pinpoints where management must change to achieve it.
