UNIVERSITY CHOICE SUBJECT DOSSIER
=================================

Subject Label: STEM University Candidate X - Bachelor Decision - 2026 Intake
System Type: University
Evaluation Date: 2026-03-21
Data Source: Program syllabus, employability stats, student feedback snapshots, tuition model

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Decide if Candidate X is a stable choice for 4-year STEM bachelor pathway.
- Decision focus: learning quality, labor-market outcomes, affordability sustainability.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. FORM EVIDENCE (Identity / Integrity over Time)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Academic core: coherent curriculum map from fundamentals to specialization.
- Faculty continuity: strong senior faculty base, but uneven assistant turnover.
- Institutional ethics: plagiarism policy active, enforcement reported as consistent.
- Research culture: good project opportunities in years 3-4.
- Accreditation posture: current accreditation valid through full study horizon.

Trend:
- Strong institutional form with moderate staffing continuity pressure.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. POSITION EVIDENCE (Context / Fit / Placement)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Industry adjacency: active internship links with regional tech employers.
- Geographic fit: close to public transport and student housing zones.
- Ecosystem position: recognized in local market, limited global brand strength.
- Peer environment: competitive cohort, mixed mentorship quality by department.
- Opportunity access: exchange programs available but slots are limited.

Trend:
- Position is strong for regional career launch, moderate for global mobility.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. ACTION EVIDENCE (Execution / Output / Energy Cost)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Graduation rate: solid completion levels in target department.
- Employability at 12 months: favorable outcomes in software/data roles.
- Student workload: high intensity, especially in semesters 3-5.
- Tuition + living cost profile: manageable with part-time work, but stress-sensitive.
- Administrative throughput: enrollment and exam administration mostly reliable.

Trend:
- Output quality is strong; energy cost is high for students with weak financial buffer.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. RISKS / HEADWINDS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Burnout risk due to high workload concentration.
- Internship quality varies by network and timing.
- Cost-of-living increases can reduce study focus and completion resilience.
- Program quality heterogeneity across departments.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. INTERVENTIONS / SCENARIOS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Scenario A - Full-time focus:
- No part-time job in first year, maximize academic adaptation.
- Expected effect: better Form/Action stability, higher short-term financial pressure.

Scenario B - Work-study blend:
- 12-16h/week part-time from semester 2.
- Expected effect: better cash flow, higher fatigue risk.

Scenario C - Hybrid + safeguards:
- Light part-time + mandatory weekly recovery and tutoring schedule.
- Expected effect: balanced sustainability and performance.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Missing: standardized long-range alumni salary distribution by track.
- Assumption: no abrupt tuition or visa policy changes during study period.
