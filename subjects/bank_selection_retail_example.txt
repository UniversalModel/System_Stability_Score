BANK SELECTION SUBJECT DOSSIER
==============================

Subject Label: Retail Bank Candidate A - Personal Finance Choice - 2026
System Type: Bank
Evaluation Date: 2026-03-21
Data Source: Public fee schedules, consumer reports, service SLAs, complaint snapshots

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Select a primary bank for salary, emergency fund, and mortgage pre-approval.
- Decision focus: trust, resilience, service reliability, hidden cost risk.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. FORM EVIDENCE (Identity / Integrity over Time)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Capital adequacy: reports indicate healthy regulatory buffers.
- Governance and compliance: no major unresolved enforcement actions in last 24 months.
- Product consistency: account and lending products stable, moderate policy drift.
- Digital architecture: mature mobile platform, periodic outage windows published.
- Transparency: fee disclosures mostly clear; some bundled terms require careful reading.

Trend:
- Structural integrity appears solid, with moderate complexity in product terms.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. POSITION EVIDENCE (Context / Fit / Placement)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Branch + ATM coverage: strong in major cities, weaker in remote areas.
- Ecosystem fit: good salary integrations and utility payment workflows.
- Market context: rate competition increased, promotional pricing is volatile.
- Customer segment alignment: strong for salaried retail clients, less attractive for freelancers.
- Cross-border usability: SEPA flows reliable, non-EU transfers less competitive.

Trend:
- Context fit is strong for domestic daily banking, mixed for international-heavy usage.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. ACTION EVIDENCE (Execution / Output / Energy Cost)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- App uptime (reported): high, with rare short degradations during peak updates.
- Support responsiveness: chat fast, branch resolution slower for complex cases.
- Transaction execution: domestic transfers reliable, card dispute handling moderate speed.
- Fee energy cost: base account low-cost, premium bundles can erode value if underused.
- Onboarding friction: digital onboarding quick, KYC escalation may add delays.

Trend:
- Day-to-day action reliability is good; complexity cost appears in edge cases.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. RISKS / HEADWINDS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Hidden fee accumulation via bundled services and inactive product add-ons.
- Service quality divergence between digital and branch channels.
- Promotional rates may revert quickly after introductory periods.
- Lower attractiveness for irregular-income profiles.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. INTERVENTIONS / SCENARIOS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Scenario A - Full switch now:
- Move salary + cards + savings immediately.
- Expected effect: faster consolidation, higher switching risk if hidden costs emerge.

Scenario B - Parallel 90-day test:
- Keep old bank, run salary and payments through Candidate A only.
- Expected effect: lower lock-in risk, better real-world service validation.

Scenario C - Split strategy:
- Candidate A for daily banking, separate institution for savings/investments.
- Expected effect: lower concentration risk, slightly higher operational overhead.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Missing: audited long-term complaint-resolution metrics by product type.
- Assumption: regulatory baseline and deposit protection remain stable.
