CITY RELOCATION SUBJECT DOSSIER
==============================

Subject Label: Sofia, Bulgaria - Relocation Decision Snapshot - Q1 2026
System Type: City
Evaluation Date: 2026-03-21
Data Source: Municipal KPIs, infrastructure notes, household budget simulation

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Evaluate whether Sofia is a stable destination for 5-year family relocation.
- Decision focus: quality of life vs cost pressure vs service reliability.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. FORM EVIDENCE (Identity / Integrity over Time)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Institutional continuity: stable municipal governance cycles, but slow permit workflows.
- Core urban structure: compact central zones + expanding ring neighborhoods.
- Utilities baseline: electricity and water coverage high; district heating aging in parts.
- Public safety trend: moderate crime profile with neighborhood variance.
- Environmental stress: winter PM pollution spikes remain a chronic issue.

Trend:
- Structural continuity is acceptable, but environmental and maintenance debt persists.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. POSITION EVIDENCE (Context / Fit / Placement)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Location advantage: strong regional connectivity (airport, highways, EU mobility).
- Labor market fit: good for IT/services, mixed for advanced manufacturing roles.
- Neighborhood suitability: south districts show better school/access trade-offs.
- Commute pressure: peak congestion high; metro improves predictability on key axes.
- External dependencies: energy price sensitivity affects household volatility.

Trend:
- Context fit is strong for knowledge workers; weaker for car-dependent daily routines.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. ACTION EVIDENCE (Execution / Output / Energy Cost)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Monthly family budget simulation (2 adults + 1 child): EUR 1,950-2,450.
- Public transport reliability: metro high reliability; bus/tram variability in rush hours.
- Healthcare access: strong private options, public waiting times uneven.
- Education access: quality public schools are geographically concentrated.
- Administrative throughput: digital services improving, but in-person processes still heavy.

Trend:
- Functional capacity is good but requires higher personal coordination energy.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. RISKS / HEADWINDS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Housing affordability pressure in high-demand districts.
- Air quality seasonality (winter) may impact long-term health comfort.
- Service quality differs significantly by neighborhood.
- Bureaucratic friction can consume time for newcomers.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. INTERVENTIONS / SCENARIOS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Scenario A - Cost-optimized relocation:
- Choose outer district + public transport dependence.
- Expected effect: lower rent, higher commute uncertainty.

Scenario B - Stability-first relocation:
- Choose metro-adjacent district with stronger school and clinic access.
- Expected effect: higher monthly cost, lower daily volatility.

Scenario C - Staged move:
- 6-month rental trial before purchase or long lease.
- Expected effect: reduced lock-in risk and better district fit.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Missing: district-level longitudinal healthcare and school outcome data.
- Assumption: no major macro shock to energy and housing markets within 12 months.
